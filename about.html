ABOUT ME

A brief history of my (currently) brief life.

I'll write this in first person, because third person feels weird.

  I was born in Texas in April of 2003, though I don't remember much before I moved to Florida. Some of my earliest memories include watching my siblings play video games such as The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, and cheering them on whenever they got past a tough section. From making Miis with my family and babysitter to even getting to try the games out myself sometimes, these early memories show how my love for video games was born during this time period.

  In seventh grade, I joined the middle school band as a clarinet player. Aside from slamming random keys on a piano and giggling at the funny noises, this was my first experience with a musical instrument. I learned the essential skill of practice, and I learned how to work with a team to make something greater than the individual. I also got my first digital piano this year, and I made some of my earliest compositions, though I had only a basic understanding of music theory.

  It was around this time that I fell in love with the soundtrack to one of my favorite games of all time: The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword (Yes, really). The soaring orchestra and colorful tones introduced me to the wonders of video game music, and I dreamt of writing similar pieces. A fascination with music theory took hold of me during my freshman year of high school, and I started dedicating most of my free time to learning all that I could about how to properly compose pieces.

  Around the time that The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild was coming out, I made one of my greatest discoveries: a YouTube channel called ClefferNotes. This artist had somehow created high quality orchestral pieces using a digital audio workstation instead of live musicians, and it fascinated me. It wasn't until next year that I would begin to try this out for myself, however. Later that year, I joined a project known as Beyond Skyrim, a fanmade project that aims to modify The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim. Since then, I've spent countless hours refining my craft and learning to properly produce pieces rather than just compose them.

  I'm still working on improving, and I don't think I'll ever stop trying to be the best composer I can be. In the midst of my college years as a BA in Music student at the University of North Texas, I'm looking to the future and making plans for my life. I hope I can turn composing for video games and films into a career someday, and I'll do whatever I can to make this dream a reality.
